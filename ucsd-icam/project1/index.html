In Fossil Shaman, you are a sorcerer who is tasked with defeating the dinosaur infestation on nearby Pale Island. Unfortunately, when you get there, you realize that your weak spells barely do any damage to the bulky, tough dinos.

Luckily, you specialize in necromancy.

This game was a concept based on my interest in both paleontology and in ghost stories. I really enjoy creating paleoart, and I wanted to use that for a concept. In the game, you revive fossils and control them to fight other living dinosaurs in turn-based combat (which, in turn, you can revive after killing). The goal of the game is to rid the island of dinos.

The Dunkleosteus’ body is mostly made of cartilage, so no recoverable fossils have been found. The reason it was such a ferocious predator, though, was because it’s head was covered in bony armor. Since that’s the only Dunkleosteus fossil found, it’s the only part you can control.

The Quetzalcoatlus is a type of pterosaur. Although it’s not actually a dinosaur, its huge size and peak allow it to deal heavy damage.

The Stegosaurus’ plates have several proposed uses, but in this game they’re used for defense. Since it was a herbivore and doesn’t have sharp teeth, the spikes on its tail, known as the “thagomizer” are the Stego’s main form of attack.
